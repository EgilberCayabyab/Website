7.Persian

Is a breed of domestic cat characterized by their long, thick coats and distinctive square heads with large ears and almond-shaped blue eyes. They are known for being friendly, sociable, and intelligent pets. Persian cats were developed in Iran (Persia) in the 17th century and remain popular around the world. They come in a variety of colors and patterns, from solid colors to calico and tortoiseshell. Persian cats are generally healthy, but they may be prone to certain genetic disorders such as respiratory issues and dental problems.

8.Sphynx

is a breed of domestic cat known for its hairlessness. Unlike other hairless cats such as the Devon and Cornish Rex, the Sphynx has no fur at all, including whiskers, eyelashes, and ear tufts. They come in a variety of colors and are often mistaken for dogs due to their unique appearance. While they may appear vulnerable, Sphynx cats are actually quite healthy and have a lifespan of 8-12 years. They require regular grooming to remove oil buildup on their skin, but otherwise make great pets for those looking for a low maintenance companion.
